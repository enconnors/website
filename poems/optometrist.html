Optometrist Poems

1.
If I was
an optometrist
I would let
the patients
pick the
music so they’d
be happy and
I’d hear
new music.
Then I’d go
home and
get drunk on
craft beer and
look at Polaroids of
my ex-wife naked and
think about killing
myself until I fell
asleep.

2.
I hope the
vision lady likes
my eyes I hope
she compliments them
I hope I’m
taller than her
husband and
the waiting room has
People and I hope
there’s no wait and
the receptionist acts
like she knows me and
guess what?
I’m taller than
her husband too.

3.
My vision lady was a
vision boy today and
kind of strange and
awkward, like a
vision intern.
The screen
where they show
you letters
broke and I said
The classic eye charts are
laughing in their
graves and he said
I know
This is what we get for our hubris.
Then he showed me
pictures and asked
Better or worse
Better or worse
Better or worse and
almost everything was
better and I always
knew right away and
I expected him to say
You’re the most
decisive patient we’ve
ever had
You know exactly
what you want
but he just
dilated my eyes and
left me
in the waiting room.
Walls full
of nice glasses.

4.
Get this
My friend from high school gets in a
bad car accident.
Loses sight in his left eye.
I say to everyone that
will listen:
Life is precious.
Sight is precious.
That night I take an edible and
marvel at the eye. I say
I think how the
eye works is it’s a
set of mirrors
like a periscope.
My friend from college says no,
it’s photoreceptors.
I ask him if it’s
true that everyone
sees different colors and
he says no,
that’s impossible.
The next morning
I go to the
eye doctor and
I’m so busy
writing poems
I forget everything
I learned the day before.
They charge me $114 and
I’m like what?
This sucks.
I’m never going to the optometrist again.
