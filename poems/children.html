Elliot Connors

My Poems Are My Children

Why the fuck am I so tired I never do anything. What does it feel like to have ambition beyond the ambition to be held. I think I’d be happier if no one else was doing anything but who knows. I hate working hard just the thought of it makes me sick. My apathy astounds and my privilege enlightens. Just a boy with too much money and not enough time or vice versa or too much of both or neither. Nothing left to attain, nothing I don’t already have save for children, but my poems are my children and all the children in the street are my children. I make faces and they run back to their parents.
