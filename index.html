Elliot Connors is a formerly depressed writer from the American Rust Belt, a formerly depressed region that, much like Elliot, has enjoyed something of a renaissance in recent years. Elliot enjoys writing about people’s mental health struggles, in the hopes that he might monetize these people’s suffering and become a messianic figure to depressed people around the globe.

Elliot's prose has been honored or featured in the Best American Essays of 2021, the Adroit Journal, the Yale Literary Magazine, McSweeney’s Internet Tendency, and the Yale Record, the world’s oldest humor publication, of which he was Editor in Chief. At Yale, he studied under Susan Choi, Anne Fadiman, and Mark Oppenheimer.

In 2019, he received a Richter Fellowship to live in rural Québec and research the curse a priest put on his family that caused 5 of his relatives to commit suicide. He is currently working on a memoir about that experience, as well as developing several television pilots and assisting an acclaimed screenwriter with his wide-ranging portfolio.

Beyond writing, Elliot works as a Senior Data Analyst for the homepage of the New York Times, and previously conducted research on decarceration for the Vera Institute of Justice.
