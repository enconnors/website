Elliot Connors

The author with his father on Facetime.

Elliot Connors is a formerly depressed writer from the American Rust Belt, a formerly depressed region that, much like Elliot, has enjoyed something of a renaissance in recent years.

Elliot enjoys writing about people’s mental health struggles, in the hopes that he might monetize these people’s suffering and become a messianic figure to depressed people around the globe.

His comedy has been published in McSweeney’s and the Yale Record, the world’s oldest humor magazine, of which he was Editor in Chief from 2018 to 2019.

His stage and screenwriting have won various minor awards, and he regularly performs sketch and improv around New York City.

In addition to comedy, Elliot is an accomplished essayist, whose work has been honored or featured in The Best American Essays of 2021, The Adroit Journal, and the Yale Literary Magazine. He is currently working on a memoir about the curse a priest put on his family in rural Québec, which caused 5 of his relatives to commit suicide.
